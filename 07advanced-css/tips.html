Wakesurfing Tips

1. Start with the Right Boat

A wakesurf-specific boat is essential. Choose one with an inboard engine and wave-shaping technology to produce clean, surfable waves.

2. Use the Correct Rope

Always use a wakesurf rope — it's shorter, thicker, and has a padded grip. Never use a traditional tow rope as it's not safe for close-distance surfing.

3. Master the Starting Position

Float with your heels on the board and knees bent. As the boat accelerates, keep your weight back until the board naturally pops under your feet.

4. Learn to Drop the Rope

Once you're in the wave's sweet spot, let go of the rope and ride freely! Shift your weight to stay in the pocket — it's all about balance and subtle movement.

5. Practice Basic Maneuvers

Work on carving back and forth and pumping to stay in the wave. These fundamentals are the key to progressing toward tricks and spins later.

6. Prioritize Safety

Always surf with a spotter on board, wear a USCG-approved life jacket, and stay clear of the boat's propeller area. Never wakesurf behind an outboard motor.
