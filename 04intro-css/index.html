Evolution of Audi

Audi’s history is rich with innovation, mergers, and remarkable engineering, making it a cornerstone of the global automotive industry.

Early Beginnings

Audi was founded in 1909 by August Horch. The name "Audi" is the Latin translation of "Horch," meaning "listen." Early on, the company was known for high-quality engineering and innovation.

The Auto Union Era

In the 1930s, Audi merged with three other manufacturers — Horch, DKW, and Wanderer — to form Auto Union. The four interlinked rings of the Audi logo represent these founding companies.

Post-War Revival

After World War II, Audi re-established itself, eventually being acquired by Volkswagen in the 1960s. The company introduced new models combining advanced technology with luxury.

Modern Innovations

Since the 1980s, Audi has become known for Quattro all-wheel drive technology, sleek designs, and advanced driver assistance systems. The brand continues to lead in electric and autonomous vehicle development.

Looking Ahead

Audi is now heavily investing in sustainability and electric mobility with models like the e-tron, aiming to reduce emissions while maintaining performance and luxury.
